# Mockup fictionalisation guide

This starter kit can be used to present public-safe website concepts and proposal-style mockups. Some source mockups may begin as real private proposal work, so they must be cleaned before they are added to a public repository or public demo.

## Keep private

Do not publish mockups that still include:

- real client or prospect logos
- real client/prospect business names
- real phone numbers or email addresses
- real addresses or service-area claims that identify the prospect
- private proposal notes
- screenshots of private conversations, estimates, or strategy notes
- copyrighted imagery that is not licensed for public portfolio use

## Safe public version

A mockup is safer for public use when it has been converted into a fictional concept:

- fictional business name
- generic or ASR-created logo
- placeholder email and phone number
- generic location or service area
- rewritten copy that explains the type of business rather than the specific prospect
- no private client notes or unpublished commercial details

## Recommended showcase format

Each public mockup should include:

```text
Title
Industry / niche
Goal of the mockup
What it demonstrates
Screenshots or live route
Short note that it is a fictional demonstration concept
```

Example:

```text
Oakline Decorating
Industry: Painting and decorating
Goal: Show a lead-generation structure for a local trades business
Demonstrates: service-area messaging, quote CTA, trust sections, mobile-first contact flow
Status: Fictional public showcase concept
```

## Practical rule

The UX thinking, section structure, layout approach, and conversion strategy can be reused.

The real identity cannot.
